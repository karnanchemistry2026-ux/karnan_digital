Physics Chapter 6

1. For a curved surface with non-uniform magnetic field, how is the magnetic flux calculated?

Your: C) By taking the average of B and multiplying by A - Correct: D) By summing the dot products of B and dA for all area elements
For curved surfaces and non-uniform fields, the surface is divided into small area elements dA. The magnetic flux through each element is B · dA, where B is the magnetic field at that element. The total flux is the sum (or integral) of all these contributions. This generalizes the simple formula for uniform fields.
Physics Chapter 6

2. What is the constant of proportionality L called in the relation NΦB = LI?

Your: C) Magnetic permeability - Correct: B) Self-inductance
The constant of proportionality L is called self-inductance of the coil. It is also called the coefficient of self-induction of the coil.
